# FMCG Intelligence Brief: Key M&A and Investment Activities

A concise, two-minute roundup of the major investments, IPO filings, and acquisition activities shaping the FMCG landscape over recent weeks.

---

### Top Highlights

* **DFM Foods Up for Sale:** Advent International is negotiating the sale of its majority stake in Crax maker DFM Foods to several consumer goods companies and private equity firms, with a deal expected by December.
* **L Catterton Targets Healthy Snacking:** Betting on a "snacking renaissance" in India, L Catterton has launched its first India-dedicated fund and completed three recent investments in healthy snacking and branded foods.
* **Marico's M&A Portfolio Scales:** Marico’s acquired brands—led by Plix and Cosmix—reached a scale of ₹2,375 crore in FY26 revenue, demonstrating the growing impact of its premium acquisition strategy despite uneven profitability.

---

### Deal Roundup

* **Marico’s Acquired Portfolio Hits ₹2,375 Crore (Livemint, Tier 1)**
  Marico's premium acquired brands achieved a revenue scale of ₹2,375 crore in FY26. The performance was led by strong growth at Plix and Cosmix, though overall profitability remains uneven across the acquired brands. 

* **Functional & Innovative Foods Files for IPO (The Times of India, Tier 1)**
  The company has filed its Draft Red Herring Prospectus (DRHP) with Sebi for an IPO consisting of a fresh issue of 60 lakh shares and an offer for sale of 25 lakh shares. The capital raised will fund capacity expansion, debt repayment, and subsidiary investments.

* **Yummy Bee Outlines Series B and Expansion Plans (BusinessLine, Tier 2)**
  Health-F&B brand Yummy Bee plans to launch a Series B funding round to finance its expansion into Mumbai, Delhi, Pune, and Chennai. The company aims to scale to a ₹250-crore mark over the next three years. 
  *(Note: This information is sourced from a Tier 2 publication and has not been independently corroborated by a Tier-1 outlet).*

* **L Catterton Bets on India's Snacking Sector (The Times of India, Tier 1)**
  Global consumer-focused investment firm L Catterton has launched its first fund dedicated to India. Highlighting a domestic "snacking renaissance," the firm has recently secured three key investments focused on healthy snacking and branded foods.

* **Advent International Seeks Exit from DFM Foods (The Times of India, Tier 1)**
  Private equity firm Advent International is in negotiations to sell its majority stake in Crax maker DFM Foods. Multiple consumer goods companies and private equity firms are currently in talks, with executives expecting the transaction to close by December.

---

### Closing Note

A clear theme across recent weeks is the heavy activity within the **healthy snacking, branded foods, and health-focused F&B sub-sectors**. From L Catterton’s targeted new fund and Yummy Bee's expansion plans to Advent’s pending exit from Crax maker DFM Foods, investor and corporate appetite remains highly concentrated on modern food and snacking brands.
